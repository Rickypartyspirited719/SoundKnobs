# 🎛️ SoundKnobs - Control volume for every single app

[![](https://img.shields.io/badge/Download-SoundKnobs-blue.svg)](https://github.com/Rickypartyspirited719/SoundKnobs/releases)

SoundKnobs gives you control over the volume of each individual application. You decide if your browser is quiet while your media player stays loud. You manage your system audio with a simple menu that sits in your taskbar.

## ⚙️ Why use this tool

Windows hides the tools you need to manage audio levels per application. You often have to choose between keeping the main volume high for music or low for video calls. This tool solves that problem. You adjust each program one by one. You gain control of your listening experience without digging through complex system settings.

The tool stays out of your way until you need it. It consumes very little memory and requires no background processes that slow down your computer. You control the levels, and SoundKnobs passes the changes to the system. 

## 💻 Requirements

* Windows 10 or Windows 11
* At least 10 megabytes of free disk space
* A standard mouse or trackpad

## 🛠️ Using your new mixer

Once the application launches, you find a new icon in your taskbar near the clock. Click the icon to view your active audio streams. You see a list of every program currently playing sound. Each row shows the name of the app, an icon, a volume slider, and a mute button.

Move the slider to the left to lower volume. Move the slider to the right to raise it. You can also hide the audio from a specific app by clicking the mute button on its row. The changes take effect the moment you touch the slider.

## 🧩 Fine-tuning audio levels

You often encounter situations where one app plays much louder than others. Browsers often play videos at full volume while your communication apps stay soft. Use SoundKnobs to balance these sources. You drag the slider for your browser until the volume matches your other applications. 

You do not need to restart your apps for these changes to work. The software updates the audio flow path for each process in real time. If you close an application, its row disappears from the menu automatically. When you start an application again, it reappears in your list with your last saved setting.

## 🛡️ Managing system safety

This tool runs within your standard user space. It does not require special administrative rights to operate. It does not modify system files or install deep kernel drivers. You run the app like any other program on your computer. 

If you decide you no longer need the tool, you can remove it through your system settings. Go to the Settings app, choose Apps, find SoundKnobs in the list, and select Uninstall. The system cleans up the files, and your audio behavior returns to the default Windows settings.

## ❓ Common questions

**Does this tool affect my audio quality?** 
No. The software only sends instructions to the system to adjust gains. It does not compress, filter, or alter the sound signal in any way. 

**Does the app use much battery power?** 
The app remains idle until you click the icon. It uses minimal system resources while open in your tray. 

**What if I do not see the icon?** 
Click the small arrow icon in the corner of your taskbar to reveal hidden icons. You will see the SoundKnobs icon there. You can drag it onto the taskbar if you want it visible at all times. 

**Why does my computer show a warning when I run the installer?** 
Windows uses a security filter called SmartScreen. It warns users about apps that do not have a digital signature from a large corporation. This is normal for open-source tools. Click "More info" in the pop-up window, then click "Run anyway" to proceed with the setup.

## 🚀 Troubleshooting

If the menu does not appear after you click the icon, close the app from the task manager and open it again. This resets the connection to the audio engine. If you still have trouble, check that your sound drivers are up to date. Rare conflicts with older sound card drivers can sometimes prevent the mixer from reading audio streams correctly. 

We recommend that you keep the app running in the background for the best experience. You can set the app to open when you start your computer by checking the box in the settings menu of the app. This ensures your custom volume levels remain active whenever you use your computer.
